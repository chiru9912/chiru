Tollywood Actors

Telugu cinema, also known as Tollywood, is the segment of Indian cinema dedicated to the production of motion pictures in Telugu language, widely spoken in the states of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. Telugu cinema is based in Film Nagar, Hyderabad.[4] As of 2021, Telugu cinema is the largest film industry in India in terms of box-office.[5][6][7] Telugu films sold 23.3 crore (233 million) tickets in 2022, the highest among various film industries in India.

The Telugu film industry originated with silent films in 1921, with the production and release of Bhishma Pratigna in 1921.[40] The film was directed by Raghupathi Venkayya Naidu and his son R. S. Prakash.[41] Y. V. Rao and R. S. Prakash established a long-lasting precedence of focusing exclusively on religious themes; Nandanar, Gajendra Moksham, and Matsyavatar, three of their most noted productions, centred on religious figures, parables, and morals.[42] The first film studio in South India, Durga Cinetone, was built in 1936 by Nidamarthi Surayya in Rajahmundry, Andhra Pradesh.[43] In 1935, Andhra Cine Tone was built in Visakhapatnam by Gottumukkala Jagannadha Raju.
